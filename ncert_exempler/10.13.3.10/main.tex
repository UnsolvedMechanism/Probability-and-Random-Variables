Question: All the jacks, queens and kings are removed from a deck of 52 playing cards. The remaining cards are well shuffled and then one card is drawn at random. Giving ace a value 1 similar value for other cards, find the probability that the card has a value 
\begin{enumerate}
	\item 7
	\item greater than 7
	\item less than 7
\end{enumerate}
\solution
Number of cards left after removing all jacks, queens and kings(=N) 
\begin{align}
	&= 52 - 4\times3\\
	&= 40
\end{align}
For any given random variable X ranging from 1 to 10, the number of cards favouring the event(n) = 4\\
$\therefore$ probabilty for the random vector, $P_{X}$ in range 1 to 10  = $\frac{n}{N} = 0.1$

\centering
\begin{tabular}{| c | c |}
	\hline
	X & $P_X$\\
	\hline
	X $<$ 7 & $\sum_{X = 1}^{6} P_X$\\ 
	\hline
	X = 7 & $P_7$\\ 
	\hline
	X $>$ 7 & $\sum_{X=8}^{10} P_X$\\ 
	\hline
\end{tabular}

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Probability to find a card with value equal to 7 
		\begin{align}
			&= P_X, X = 7\\
			&= 0.1
		\end{align}
	\item Probability to find a card with value greater than 7  
		\begin{align}
			&= \sum_{X=8}^{10}P_X \\
			&= 3\times0.1\\
			&=0.3
		\end{align}
	\item Probability to find a card with value lesser than 7 
		\begin{align}
			&= \sum_{X = 1}^{6}P_X \\
			&= 6\times0.1\\
			&=0.6
		\end{align}
\end{enumerate}
